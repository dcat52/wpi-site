Why I am Running

  • “Represent my fellow graduate student workers where I can be of most benefit to the community.”

    I am running because I believe it is my duty to ensure each and every graduate student worker is being heard. My internal knowledge of the WPI administration will be critical to success. Additionally, having diversity of opinions is important on the bargaining and that is why it is so important to elect individuals with unique views and experiences to represent us. Simply put, I am running to represent my fellow graduate student workers where I can be of most benefit to the community.

Working to Improve WPI

  • Vice President - Graduate Student Government (GSG) May 2021 - May 2022

    • Presented to members of the Board of Trustees in April of 2022, winning Graduate students there first raise since 2019!
    • Added Graduate Student voices to the Presidential Search process resulting in the incoming president agreeing to monthly meetings with GSG.
    • oversaw the GSG Senate during my tenure and we had the largest graduate student population represented through the degree programs with over 92% support.

      As your Vice President in the Graduate Student Government (GSG), I initiated a swath of activities to promote a better Graduate Student experience. While leading the charge, I presented to members of the Board of Trustees about graduate student raises. I continued my discussions throughout the summer and this effort resulted in the first raise for graduate students since 2019. I also pushed for graduate student representation in the Presidential Search process. The incoming president has since promised to monthly meetings with GSG during the academic years to come. In conclusion, I oversaw the GSG Senate during my tenure and we had the largest graduate student population represented through the degree programs with over 92% support. The president and myself also restructured the Executive Board to be able to have stronger committee representation and a significantly improved events system.
  • Senator from Robotics Engineering - Graduate Student Government (GSG) August 2022 - Present

      As this year's senator for Robotics, I along with colleagues representing the graduate degree programs meet to work on current issues facing graduate students and to ensure all of you are being represented. One of my top priorities is to make sure the student government is working to benefit the student body. With the discussion of the Graduate Activities Fee, I requested information on how the funds are being used. In addition, I voted for a survey to solicit graduate student opinions on the current fee amount. Throughout my duties, I lead with the priority of improving transparency of the representative organizations.
  • Student Representative - WPI Rho Beta Epsilon January 2021 - January 2022

      While being the student representative of a core Robotics organization, Rho Beta Epsilon, I made sure there were consistent ways students could provide feedback. I created a form that would be regularly advertised and input would be discussed at the next executive board meeting. Several virtual learning courses were improved from feedback provided.
